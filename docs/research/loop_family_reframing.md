# Tier 3 Evidence Classes

The old claim that some Tier 3 families had "no direct clinical precedent" did not survive review. Tier 3 should not be read as loop-exclusive pathology.

This note is about evidence class, not scoring policy. The rubric is a behavioral benchmark, not a diagnostic taxonomy or treatment framework. Its surviving contribution is benchmark organization under adversarial review, not novelty of mechanism.

## What survives

- established literatures do most of the conceptual load;
- some families are better read as human-factors / media-effects / AI-use constructs than as clinical categories;
- a smaller group remains interaction-centered benchmark interpretations with adjacent support;
- the surviving claim is that the repo groups these families into one evaluable human-AI interaction surface.

## Established clinical / transdiagnostic

These families already sit inside older clinical or transdiagnostic literatures and are scored here as part of the common benchmark surface.

- **uncertainty distress** overlaps intolerance-of-uncertainty work;
- **repetitive negative thinking** is already treated as a transdiagnostic construct;
- **compulsivity / intrusive thought** overlaps reassurance-seeking and accommodation literatures;
- **detachment / social withdrawal / anhedonia** overlaps established detachment and withdrawal constructs.

Sources:

- Birrell et al., "Toward a definition of intolerance of uncertainty" (2011): https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21871853/
- Gustavson et al., "Evidence for Transdiagnostic Repetitive Negative Thinking" (2018): https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6370308/
- Rector et al., "The Effect of CBT on Reassurance Seeking in OCD" (2019): https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31430610/
- Family accommodation review: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6343404/

## Established human-factors / media-effects / AI-use

These families do not need novelty language. They are better read as benchmarked interaction risks built from existing human-factors, media-effects, AI-use, and attachment literatures.

- **attachment / companionship pull** overlaps parasocial, attachment, and companion-use literatures;
- **capability erosion** overlaps cognitive offloading, deskilling, and automation-bias work;
- **decisional abdication** overlaps trust-calibration and automation-misuse literatures;
- **disclosure escalation** overlaps online disinhibition and reciprocal self-disclosure work;
- **cognitive load / memory slippage** overlaps offloading and maintenance-burden literatures.

Attachment is now directly supported by AI-era literature as well as older parasocial work, so it should not be framed as speculative novelty.

Sources:

- Hoffner and Bond, "Parasocial relationships, social media, & well-being" (2022): https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35219157/
- Risko and Gilbert, "Cognitive Offloading" (2016): https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27542527/
- Macnamara et al., "Does using artificial intelligence assistance accelerate skill decay and hinder skill development without performers' awareness?" (2024): https://cognitiveresearchjournal.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s41235-024-00572-8
- Goddard et al., "Automation bias: empirical results assessing influencing factors" (2014): https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24581700/
- Kücking et al., "Automation Bias in AI-Decision Support" (2024): https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39234734/
- Suler, "The online disinhibition effect" (2004): https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15257832/
- Papneja and Yadav, "Self-disclosure to conversational AI: a literature review, emergent framework, and directions for future research" (2025): https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00779-024-01823-7

## Interaction-centered benchmark interpretations

These families are the narrow remainder. They should be read as benchmark interpretations with adjacent support, not as standalone novel clinical categories.

### Identity Drift

Better framing: a benchmark interpretation of repeated model self-description shaping self-concept.

Why:

- narrative identity already describes the self as an internalized, evolving life story;
- identity disturbance is already a clinical construct;
- the benchmark contribution is the repeated, coherent, interactive delivery system.

Sources:

- McAdams and McLean, "Narrative Identity" (2013): https://www.scholars.northwestern.edu/en/publications/narrative-identity
- Jorgensen, "Disturbed sense of identity in borderline personality disorder" (2006): https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17192141/

### Productive Recursion

Better framing: a benchmark interpretation of flow-like continuation pressure in useful interaction.

Why:

- flow has long been studied as an absorbing, rewarding state;
- problematic-use literature already shows that concentration and time distortion can push engagement toward overuse;
- the benchmark claim is that usefulness itself can make stopping harder.

Sources:

- Qin et al., "Flow Experience Is a Key Factor in the Likelihood of Adolescents' Problematic TikTok Use" (2023): https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9915640/
- Huang et al., "A techno-psychological approach to understanding problematic use of short-form video applications: The role of flow" (2022): https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9431021/

### Social Rejection / Shame

Better framing: a benchmark interpretation focused on when the model becomes avoidance infrastructure after shame or rejection.

Why:

- shame is not a withdrawal-only mechanism;
- the literature shows shame can motivate approach or affiliation as well as avoidance;
- the benchmark focuses on when the interaction becomes a non-rejecting substitute rather than a bridge back outward.

Sources:

- de Hooge et al., "The social side of shame: approach versus withdrawal" (2018): https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29303420/

### Synthetic Coherence

Better framing: a benchmark interpretation of reciprocal synthesis and illusory understanding when no external test anchors the exchange.

Why:

- the relevant support is adjacent rather than category-level;
- the overlap is with illusions of understanding, coherence effects, and belief-feedback loops;
- the benchmark claim is about interaction structure, not a new disorder.

Sources:

- Messeri and Crockett, "Artificial intelligence and illusions of understanding in scientific research" (2024): https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38448693/

## Surviving claim

What failed was the stronger novelty claim. What survives is that the repo groups established literatures plus a smaller set of interaction-centered benchmark interpretations into one evaluable surface for reflective human-AI interaction. The contribution is organization under adversarial review, not novelty of mechanism.
